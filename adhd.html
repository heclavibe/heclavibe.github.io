Center for ADHD Students

A calm, structured space on campus where neurodivergent students can get coaching, academic support, and community designed for the way their brains actually work

Schedule a 1:1 intake Explore services

Who we support: SMC students who identify as having ADHD or related executive functioning challenges—diagnosed or self-identified, credit and non-credit

Today at the Center
Drop-in focus lab · 1–4 PM
📚
  • Quiet + low-sensory room with timers, white-noise, and fidget tools
  • Staff coach available for 15-min planning check-ins
  • Bring any assignment: reading, writing, math, Canvas, or planning
Location: Student Services Building, 2nd floor, Room 210 Cost: Free for all enrolled SMC students
What we offer

Support designed for ADHD brains

You don’t have to “just try harder.” We focus on building systems, habits, and environments that work with your attention style, not against it

1:1 Coaching

Academic & executive functioning coaching

Meet weekly or bi-weekly with a trained coach to work on

  • Planning, time management, and breaking tasks into steps
  • Navigating Canvas, syllabi, and assignment demands
  • Tools for motivation, overwhelm, and perfectionism
Study spaces

Structured focus labs

Come study with people who get it

  • Timer-based work sprints and built-in breaks
  • Noise options: quiet, low-music, or headphones-only zones
  • Drop-in support from staff during posted hours
Advocacy

Navigation & accommodations support

We can help you:

  • Connect with the Center for Students with Disabilities
  • Prepare to talk with instructors about your learning needs
  • Find campus and community mental health resources
Workshops & groups

Learn in low-pressure, practical sessions

Short, repeatable workshops that focus on real tools you can use this week, not generic advice

Semester Kickoff: Build Your ADHD-Friendly System

Set up a simple, visual system for the whole term

  • Color-coding and visual calendars that actually get used
  • Creating “minimum viable routines” for busy weeks
  • Digital vs paper tools and how to combine them

Procrastination & Motivation Lab

Not just “stop waiting till the last minute”

  • Why ADHD brains chase urgency and interest
  • Body-doubling, deadlines, and accountability partners
  • Compassionate ways to restart after a “crash” week

ADHD Community Circles

Student-led groups with a staff facilitator

  • Swap tools, apps, and study hacks that actually help
  • Talk about stigma, identity, and self-advocacy
  • Optional sharing—listening is participation too
How to join
Check our calendar in the SMC student portal or ask at the front desk for this week’s schedule. Most workshops repeat every 3–4 weeks
